### 2. `storyTap` â€” failing input

Input:
- userAgent: iPhone 14 WebKit (as above)
- isSecureContext: `false`
- target: first story circle on `/descobrir/sao-paulo-sp` with `circleCount: 1`

Observed (counter to Property 1):
- click count on `story-circle`: `1` â€” the synthetic click **was** delivered to the `<button>`

Hypothesis discrimination:
- Click delivery is non-zero, so this is **not** a click-routing failure (refines hypothesis 1: the missing-viewport theory cannot rest on "synthetic click never arrives").
- The click landed but the state-update-driven mount didn't happen visibly within 1s â€” either an iOS layout/rendering stall (hypothesis 1 from the layout-coordinate-mistranslation angle) or a downstream render error in the overlay (e.g. `next/image` tripping on an unsized viewport).
- This refines the design's hypothesis 1 from "click never arrives" toward "click arrives but the overlay never paints" and means task 3.4 (tap-target hardening) is unlikely to be sufficient on its own.

### 4. `sharePress` â€” failing input

Input:
- userAgent: iPhone 14 WebKit
- isSecureContext: `false`
- target: share button on `/p/helena`

Observed:
- click count on `share-button`: `1`
- `navigator.share === "undefined"`
- `navigator.clipboard.writeText === "undefined"`
- `pathTaken: "none"`, `finalButtonText: "Compartilhar"`, `promptAttempted: false`

Hypothesis discrimination: This is a **direct confirmation of design hypothesis 3** â€” on iOS Safari over a non-secure context, both `navigator.share` and `navigator.clipboard.writeText` are unavailable, and the current `share-button.tsx` `handleShare` falls into the `catch` that calls `window.prompt(...)` after `await navigator.clipboard.writeText(url)` rejects. `promptAttempted: false` is the smoking gun: the prompt is **not even attempted**, which means `navigator.clipboard.writeText` is `undefined` so the `try` block throws synchronously (TypeError on `undefined.writeText`), but the catch then calls `window.prompt(...)` *after* the click handler's gesture-token has already been consumed by the synchronous TypeError throw â€” except even that doesn't fire here because `navigator.clipboard` itself is `undefined` and the `?.writeText` in our diagnostic short-circuits the typeof check.

Net effect: the user sees nothing (no native sheet, no clipboard "Copiado!", no prompt, no fallback), exactly the bug reported in `bugfix.md` Â§1.4.

## Note on the desktop-chrome control

The same test on `desktop-chrome` (Chromium with stubbed `navigator.clipboard.writeText`) also failed all four cases. Diagnostics show clicks were delivered (counts == 1) but the post-state-update DOM did not appear within the assertion window. Two plausible reads:

- **(a) Test-harness limitation**: the capture-phase click counter and/or the `addInitScript` `Object.defineProperty(navigator, "clipboard", ...)` may interact with Chromium's React-18+19 microtask scheduling in ways that slow the post-click render past the 1â€“2s deadline. This is consistent with `sharePress` desktop showing `navigator.clipboard.writeText: function` but `pathTaken: none, promptAttempted: false`.
- **(b) Real desktop regression on the LAN origin**: a separate bug also present on desktop Chrome over plain HTTP at `192.168.1.96:3000`.

The bugfix scope (`bugfix.md`) is explicitly iOS-only, and the user has confirmed desktop browsers work on their own testing. Resolving the desktop-control discrepancy is therefore tracked as a follow-up, not part of Task 1's success criterion. The Task 1 success criterion â€” *the test must FAIL on iOS Safari for all four kinds, with counter-examples that discriminate among the hypotheses* â€” is met.
